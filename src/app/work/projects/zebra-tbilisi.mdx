## Overview

For Zebra Tbilisi, a creative agency that began as a clothing line and has grown into a global artistic platform, I developed a website to reflect their unique brand identity. The design, provided through Figma, presented distinct challenges that required custom development from the ground up. The site is a multimedia hub, showcasing their collaborations with international artists and hosting original videography and creative content.

The site had to be adaptable to Zebra's evolving needs, such as multilingual content, custom layouts, and seamless integration of their creative projects, from merchandise to events.

## Key Features

- **Custom-Built UI**: Due to Zebra's unconventional design, I built every element from scratch, avoiding standard UI component libraries to deliver a highly personalized user experience.
- **Dynamic Content Management**: Integrated with Nuxt Content for easy management of multimedia, including music videos, short films, and blog posts, allowing the team to update content without developer intervention.
- **Multi-language Support**: Implemented i18n for seamless localization, allowing Zebra's global audience to access the site in multiple languages.
- **SEO Optimization**: Utilized Nuxt SEO Kit to ensure that Zebra's content reaches the right audience across different regions, with optimized meta tags, structured data, and page performance enhancements.

## Technologies Used

- **Nuxt3**: For building a fast, server-rendered site that supports Zebra's global audience.
- **Tailwind CSS**: Used for creating fully custom and responsive designs that align with Zebra's unconventional visual identity.
- **Pinia**: State management to handle global data like user preferences and language selection.
- **Nuxt Content**: Enabled dynamic content management for articles, event posts, and video releases.
- **Nuxt SEO Kit**: To ensure optimal search engine performance and reach.
- **i18n**: For supporting multiple languages and catering to Zebra’s diverse audience.

## Challenges and Learnings

The primary challenge was the non-traditional design language. Unlike typical websites that rely on UI libraries, Zebra’s site required fully custom components to achieve the intended look and feel. This pushed the boundaries of my front-end development skills, especially in ensuring a smooth, responsive experience without using pre-built components.

Balancing aesthetics with performance was another challenge. Custom layouts and media content often increased complexity, but I optimized every component to ensure fast load times, even for Zebra’s multimedia content.

## Outcome

The website now serves as a central hub for Zebra Tbilisi's global creative initiatives, enabling them to reach a wider audience while maintaining their distinctive visual style. The result is a site that both reflects the unique spirit of the brand and offers an engaging experience for users across the globe.
